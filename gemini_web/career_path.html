Build Your Roadmap

Once you have an idea of the path you want to pursue, the next step is to plan your journey. This section outlines different learning strategies and the essential steps to take when you're ready to start your job search. A solid plan is key to a successful career transition.

1. Choose Your Learning Method

University Degree

A traditional path providing a deep theoretical foundation. Best for those starting out or seeking roles in research or academia. Can be time-consuming and expensive.

Coding Bootcamps

Intensive, career-focused programs designed to get you job-ready in 3-9 months. They offer structured curriculum and career support but require a significant time and financial commitment.

Online Courses & Self-Study

A flexible and affordable option using platforms like Coursera, Udemy, or freeCodeCamp. Requires strong self-discipline and motivation to build a curriculum and stay on track.

2. Land Your First Tech Job

1
Build a Portfolio

Create 2-3 high-quality projects that showcase your skills. This is your proof of ability, often more important than a resume for entry-level roles.

2
Craft Your Resume

Tailor your resume to tech roles. Highlight transferable skills from your previous career, such as problem-solving, project management, and communication.

3
Network Strategically

Engage with people in the industry online (LinkedIn, Twitter) and at local meetups. Networking can lead to mentorship and job referrals.

4
Prepare for Interviews

Practice common technical questions (e.g., LeetCode for developers) and be ready to talk in-depth about your portfolio projects and behavioral questions.
